# 📊 Social Pulse: Student Social Media & Wellbeing Analytics

## 📖 Project Overview

Social media has become an inseparable part of students' daily lives.

But an important question remains:

> How does social media usage influence students' wellbeing, academic performance, sleep quality, and relationships?

To answer this question, I built an interactive **Power BI dashboard** that transforms raw student survey data into meaningful insights.

Rather than simply creating charts, the objective was to build a dashboard that helps users identify behavioral patterns and make informed decisions through data.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Educational institutions, parents, and policymakers often struggle to understand:

- How much time students spend on social media
- Whether excessive screen time affects academic performance
- The relationship between sleep and productivity
- Mental wellbeing trends
- Platform usage behavior
- Relationship conflicts influenced by social media

This dashboard provides a data-driven approach to answering these questions.

---

- ✔️ Analyze social media usage patterns

## 📈 Key Business Insights

### 📱 Social Media Usage
- Students spend an average of 4.92 hours/day on social media.
- Heavy usage indicates strong digital dependency.

### 😴 Sleep & Productivity
- Average sleep duration is only 6.87 hours.
- This suggests that increased screen time may contribute to reduced sleep quality and potentially impact productivity.

### 🎓 Academic Behaviour

###  Slide 2 — Mental Health & Lifestyle Analysis



<img width="1045" height="641" alt="617068519-fc0daaeb-e503-4554-b551-451cb962ca72" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4150d42a-c017-4ec8-9018-8d805db3ccd4" />



---

**Business Objective**

The objective of this page is to analyze how social media addiction relates to students' mental wellbeing, sleep patterns, and overall lifestyle.

Instead of looking at isolated metrics, this page focuses on identifying relationships between multiple wellbeing indicators.

---

**Visuals Explained**


*Addiction Score vs Mental Health Score*
A scatter plot compares students' addiction scores with their mental health ratings. Rather than displaying averages, this visualization highlights behavioral patterns across individual students.



**Finding:** Students with higher addiction scores generally tend to report lower mental health ratings, indicating a possible negative relationship between excessive social media usage and wellbeing.



*Average Sleep Hours by Age*
This line chart compares sleep duration across different age groups.



**Finding:** Most students average between 6.8 and 7 hours of sleep, which is slightly below the recommended duration for healthy adults.

*Country-wise Health Band*
Students are grouped into different health categories based on wellbeing scores. This visualization enables geographical comparisons and highlights variations in mental wellbeing across regions.

---


**Key Insights**

- Higher addiction scores are generally associated with lower mental wellbeing.
- Students consistently report sleep durations below the recommended range.
- Mental wellbeing patterns vary across countries and student groups.

---

**Business Value**


This page provides a high-level overview for stakeholders, allowing them to quickly identify areas of concern before exploring detailed analyses. It supports strategic decision-making by presenting the most critical metrics in a single view.


Educational institutions can use these insights to identify students who may benefit from digital wellbeing initiatives, counseling programs, and awareness campaigns.
